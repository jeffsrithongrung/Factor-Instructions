How to use the Claude project to write, scale, and ideate Factor creative.

v3.0 · April 2026 · System v6.1

The 3-Variable Gate

Claude will not generate hooks, scripts, or concepts until you confirm three things. If you skip any, it will ask before proceeding. This is the single most important thing to know.

Required in every first message
1. Lane Which marketing lane or audience angle? (Protein, Mediterranean, GLP-1, Convenience, Mom, Dad, Skeptic, Salad, Fiber, Breakfast, Weight Loss)

New lane not listed? That's fine — name it anyway (e.g. "seed oil," "couples," "cooking for one"). Claude will treat it as a NEW job and use generative methods. No existing assets required.
2. Funnel Stage FF (Full Funnel) — hook earns cold attention, body builds conviction. Hook cannot name Factor. ~60% of sprint output.
MoF (Mid Funnel) — viewer already knows Factor. Skip product education, go straight to conviction. Hook can name Factor. ~40% of sprint output.
3. Job Type MORE — controlled variants of a winner (same lane, same audience)
ADAPT — transplant a winner's mechanism to a new lane/context
UPGRADE — improve salvageable creative
NEW — net-new exploration
Rule of thumb: Constrained briefs beat broad ones every time. "Fiber lane, Structure B, FF, Mistake/Warning hook" will produce sharper output than "write me a good script."
New or low-coverage lanes: Claude works with any lane — even ones with zero library assets. For lanes with fewer than 3 existing assets, Claude will flag that confidence is lower and treat concepts as hypotheses for testing. It uses three generative methods (Problem-First, Cultural Moment, Competitive Gap) instead of scaling from winners. Use NEW as your job type and the Ideate workflow.

What Claude Does First

After you pass the gate, Claude's first response depends on the job type:

Job Type First Response
WRITE 5–8 hooks with 1-line rationale each, archetype tagged. No strategy preamble.
SCALE Decomposition of your source asset (hook mechanism, structural engine, copy engine, audience contract), then 3–5 variant directions.
IDEATE 3 white space opportunities with trade-offs. Hooks come after you pick a direction.

Write Workflow

For new scripts, hooks, and concepts. Use when you have an angle but not a source asset to scale from.

1
Open with the gate
State your lane, funnel stage (FF or MoF), and job type (WRITE/NEW). Add structure or hook archetype constraints if you have preferences — constraints sharpen output.
2
Review the hooks
Claude returns 5–8 hooks with archetype tags and 1-line rationale. It flags which push fresh territory vs. iterate on proven patterns. Pick your favorites or redirect.
3
Develop the copy arc
Once you pick hook(s), Claude writes the full copy arc: onramp → benefit sequence → barrier-bust → CTA. No timestamps or visual direction yet — that comes later.
4
Iterate on the script
Give feedback. Claude adjusts. After each round you'll see a quality scorecard — three scores out of 5 (Hook, Onramp, Benefit Sequence). The weakest dimension gets a specific fix recommendation.
5
Format for production
When you're ready to brief production, ask for the formatted script: timestamps, visuals, copy, and VO on separate lines. Claude only does this when you ask.
The quality scorecard appears after every script. Format:
Hook: 4/5 → #28 | Onramp: 3/5 → #18 | Benefit: 4/5 → #33
Anchor citations (e.g. → #28) ground each score against a proven performer. If total is below 10/15, Claude automatically diagnoses the problem.

Scale Workflow

For MORE (variants of a winner) and ADAPT (transplant to new lane/context). Always starts with decomposition — skipping this is the #1 cause of weak scaling output.

1
Open with the gate + source asset
State lane, funnel, and job type (MORE or ADAPT). Name the source asset by number or nickname. Example: "MORE on #23 (Recovery Meal Hack), FF, Protein lane."
2
Review the decomposition
Claude breaks down the source into four components:
• Hook mechanism — what psychological lever the first 2s pulls
• Structural engine — the persuasion sequence beat by beat
• Copy engine — the repeating language pattern or rhetorical device
• Audience contract — who it promises value to and what value
It names what to preserve vs. change.
3
Review scaled variants
3–5 variants presented as hook + copy arc (no formatting). Each tagged. For MORE, core audience stays the same — variants change hooks, openers, format. For ADAPT, the mechanism transfers to a new lane or context.
4
Pick, iterate, format
Same as Write steps 4–5. Pick direction, iterate with scorecard, request production format when ready.
Hook-swap warning: Assets #15/#14, #21/#16, and #41/#13 share body footage. Don't cite them as independent creative or claim new Entity IDs. They're useful for understanding which hooks outperform on the same body.

Feeding Claude Ads Outside the Library

To scale, adapt, or analyze an ad that isn't in the 42-asset library, provide one of these:

Best Scene-by-scene breakdown from Gemini (see Video Breakdown tab)
Good VO transcript — Claude can analyze the copy engine and persuasion arc from words alone
Workable Text description of what happens, who's in it, what's shown, how long it runs

Ideate Workflow

For NEW concepts and white space exploration. Use when you want to push into underserved territory rather than iterate on what exists.

1
Open with the gate
State lane (or "open — show me the gaps"), funnel stage, and job type (NEW/IDEATE). Claude defaults to including at least one MoF concept.
2
Review white space opportunities
Claude surfaces 3 opportunities in your lane with rationale — what's underserved, what the library is missing, what has high probability of differentiating. Each comes with trade-offs.
3
Pick a territory
Choose which white space direction to explore — or redirect Claude if none land.
4
Review hooks
5–8 hooks generated for your chosen territory. For IDEATE/NEW, Claude uses structured generative methods (Problem-First, Cultural Moment, Competitive Gap) and tags which method produced each hook.
5
Develop, iterate, format
Same as Write steps 3–5.

Steering Phrases

These exact phrases trigger specific behaviors. Use them mid-conversation to redirect output without re-explaining what you want.

Quality Controls

"Show me your filter" Switches to full rubric mode — all candidates with complete scores and reasoning across all 3 dimensions.
"Just give me your best" Returns to scorecard mode — only the top pick with compact scores.

Direction Changes

"Too safe" / "Push harder" Claude shifts to lower-saturation hook archetypes. May switch to Structure B if you're in D.
"Too generic" Claude adds specific numbers, names specific behaviors, mines CSV VOs for authentic phrasing.
"More like Asset #X" Claude decomposes #X (hook mechanism, structural engine, copy engine, audience contract) and applies that mechanism to your current concept.
"Not converting" / "Not DR enough" Tightens benefit sequence. Relief before features. More barrier-busting. Sharper CTA.
"Feels like an ad" First-person voice. Removes brand language from first 10 seconds. Shifts to confessional or situational hooks.

Session Management

Start a new chat After ~25 exchanges, output quality degrades (context fatigue). Open a fresh conversation for sustained work. This is the correct workflow, not a workaround.
Reference an asset by number Claude has the full 42-asset library loaded. Say "#23" or "Recovery Meal Hack" — no need to paste transcripts for library assets.

What Claude Won't Do Until You Ask

Full script formatting Timestamps + visual + copy + VO format only appears when you request it. Default output is hooks → copy arc.
Visual direction Claude writes copy and VO first. Ask for visual direction when you're ready to brief production.
Duration estimates Available on request or when you ask for a production brief.

Fix Bad Output

When Claude's output isn't landing, use this diagnostic map. The system has a built-in corrections file — these phrases help you trigger the right fix faster.

Script Problems

Symptom What's Wrong Say This
Sounds like an ad / voice breaks mid-script Product Page Break. Script shifted from authentic first-person to brand voice in the benefit sequence. "This sounds like a product page. Rewrite the benefit sequence through the character's experience."
Fine but flat / scores 8–9 range Missing Center of Gravity. No single line reorganizes how the viewer thinks about their behavior. "Where's the reframe? I need one line that changes how the viewer sees their own situation."
Strong hook, weak body Front-Loaded Script. Onramp resolved the tension instead of deepening it. "The hook peaked at second two. The onramp needs to deepen investment, not deliver answers."
Generic / could be any brand Abstraction Drift. Operating at category level, not behavior level. "Too generic — add specific numbers, name specific behaviors, name the alternative they're currently using."
Viewer has no reason to believe Thin Barrier-Busting. Objections addressed with generic assertions, not specific evidence. "The barrier-bust is decorative. Name the specific objection this persona has and answer it with evidence."

Funnel Problems

Symptom What's Wrong Say This
MoF script explains what Factor is Script re-educates a viewer who already knows. Re-explaining signals the ad isn't for them. "This is MoF — strip all product education. No 'fresh never frozen,' no 'here's how it works.' Skip to conviction."
Script is longer than it needs to be Adding persuasion beats for a viewer who doesn't need persuading. "This viewer is warm — what beat exists that they don't already know? Cut it."

Session Problems

Symptom What's Wrong Fix
Output getting worse over time Context Fatigue. After ~25 exchanges, Claude's attention to project files degrades. Start a new conversation. This is not a workaround — it's the correct workflow.
Iterating without improvement Wrong Unit of Iteration. Refining language when structure is the problem, or refining hooks when the onramp is weak. Ask: "What's the lowest-scoring dimension? Iterate on that, not the thing I asked about."
Everything is generic Vague Brief. Broad requests produce safe output. Add constraints. Specify structure + archetype + persona + lane. Constraints are creative forcing functions.

Video Breakdown (Gemini)

Use this prompt in Gemini to create scene-by-scene breakdowns of video ads. The output format matches what Claude expects for SCALE and analysis work.

When to use: When you want Claude to scale, adapt, or analyze an ad that isn't in the 42-asset library. Upload the video to Gemini, run this prompt, then paste the output into Claude.
You are analyzing a direct-response video advertisement for a meal delivery service. Provide a detailed scene-by-scene breakdown using the following format. IMPORTANT: Break scenes by CONTENT BEATS (when the message, visual focus, or argument changes), NOT by fixed time intervals. A scene can be 1 second or 8 seconds — the break happens when the ad shifts what it's doing. For each scene: [Timestamp range] Visual: What's on screen — setting, talent, product shots, text overlays, transitions Copy: Any on-screen text (exact wording) VO: Voiceover script (exact wording) Function: What this beat is DOING in the persuasion sequence (hook, pain point, solution entry, feature proof, barrier-bust, social proof, variety showcase, CTA, etc.) After the scene breakdown, provide: METADATA: - Estimated duration: [Xs] - Primary Structure: [A (Narrative), B (Listicle), C (Rapid Proof), or D (Testimonial)] - Hook Archetype: [type] - Primary Lane: [which marketing angle] - Motivator: [M-PROBLEM, M-OUTCOME, M-DEAL, or M-ANTI-ALT] - Funnel Stage: [FF or MoF] - Talent: [description — gender, approximate age, setting] - Production Style: [studio/lifestyle/UGC-style/mixed] Be precise with timestamps and exact wording. When in doubt about a word, flag it with [?].

Tips

Use Gemini 2.5 Pro — it handles video natively. Upload the video file directly.

Beat-based, not time-based. This prompt breaks scenes by content shifts, not fixed intervals. A 2-second hook and an 8-second benefit sequence are both one "scene."

Paste the full output into Claude. Claude will use the breakdown for decomposition, scaling, and analysis. The metadata tags match Claude's tagging system.
