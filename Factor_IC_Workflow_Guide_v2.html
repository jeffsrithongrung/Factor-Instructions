IC Workflow Guide

How to use the Factor Claude project. Tell it what you want, steer the output, get production-ready scripts.

Three things before you start

Every session starts with three inputs. State them in your first message and Claude skips the intake questions and goes straight to work.

Job type
MORE — Scale a winner. Controlled variants.
ADAPT — Transplant a winning mechanism into a new lane or context.
UPGRADE — Fix a near-miss. Diagnose and rewrite the weak spot.
NEW — Net-new concept. Highest risk, biggest upside.
Lane
Protein / Fitness — HIGH saturation
Mediterranean — HOT, moderate
GLP-1 — Expanding
Convenience — Moderate
Busy Mom — Moderate
Fiber / Gut Health — LOW (big gap)
Breakfast — LOW (big gap)
+ more: Weight Loss, Skeptic, Salad, Busy Dad
Funnel stage
Cold — Viewer has never heard of Factor. Full education + proof needed.
Mid — Viewer knows Factor exists. Social proof + identity + objection handling.
Full — Viewer is warm. Short, conviction-forward, offer matters more.
Example opener: "NEW concept, GLP-1 lane, cold funnel, Structure B" — that's enough context for Claude to skip all intake and deliver hooks immediately.

What happens if you don't specify?

Claude will ask you. It won't generate hooks, scripts, or concepts until it knows all three. This isn't Claude being difficult — funnel stage alone changes the entire script architecture. Specifying upfront saves you a round trip.

Seven steps, every session

Regardless of job type, the flow is the same. What changes is what you get at Step 2.

Step 1
State your intent
Job type + lane + funnel stage in your first message. Add structure, archetype, or persona constraints if you have them. The more specific, the better the first batch.

"ADAPT #20 into Mediterranean, cold funnel"
"NEW concept, Breakfast lane, cold, Structure B"
"MORE variants of #38, same lane, mid-funnel"
Step 2
Get your starting material
What Claude gives you depends on the job type:
Write / NEW
5–8 hooks, each tagged with archetype. Fresh vs. proven flagged. No strategy essays.
MORE / ADAPT
Decomposition of the source asset (hook mechanism, structural engine, copy engine, audience contract) + 3–5 variant directions.
IDEATE
3 white space territories with rationale and trade-offs. No hooks yet — pick a direction first.
Step 3
Pick direction
Choose 1–2 hooks or a territory. If nothing lands, say what's missing — Claude regenerates against your feedback, not from scratch.

"Love 3 and 6, develop both"
"None of these are fresh enough — push harder"
"Territory 2, let's see hooks"
Step 4
Copy arc develops
Claude writes the full selling sequence: onramp → benefit sequence → barrier-bust → CTA. No timestamps, no visuals yet. This is copy only — you're evaluating the persuasion arc, not the production brief.
Step 5
Steer and refine
Use directional phrases to redirect. Claude maps these to specific adjustments:

"Too safe" → shifts to lower-saturation archetypes, cultural moment territory
"Too generic" → adds numbers, named situations, viewer behaviors
"More like #22" → decomposes that asset's mechanism and applies it
"Not converting" → tightens benefit sequence, adds barrier-busting
"Feels like an ad" → first-person voice, removes brand language from first 10s
Step 6
Quality check
Every script gets a scorecard automatically — three dimensions scored 1–10:

Hook freshness · Benefit sequence · Pacing & economy

If you want the full 5-dimension breakdown with all candidates scored: "show me your filter"
To go back to just seeing the best output: "just give me your best"
Step 7
Format for production
Only when you're ready. Say one of these and Claude adds timestamps, visual direction, and shot-level detail:

"Format this"
"Ready to brief"
"Add visuals"
Don't skip steps. Jumping from Step 1 straight to "write me a full script" forces Claude to make assumptions about direction. Hooks first → copy arc → production format. Each step is a decision point where you steer.

When output drops

Claude has a diagnostic system for quality problems. These are the symptoms you'll feel and the phrases that trigger the right fix.

The symptom
"This sounds like an ad"
Say this
Say exactly that. Claude diagnoses a product page break — the script shifted from authentic voice to brand copy mid-stream. It rewrites features as character experiences.
The symptom
"It's fine but it's flat"
Say this
"This is functional but there's no center of gravity" — Claude identifies the missing reframe line that should reorganize how the viewer thinks about their own behavior.
The symptom
"Strong hook, weak body"
Say this
"The back half falls apart" or "hook is strong but the body isn't landing" — Claude diagnoses a front-loaded script where the onramp resolved tension too early.
The symptom
"Too generic / could be any brand"
Say this
"Too generic" or "not specific enough" — Claude injects concrete behaviors, precise numbers, and names the specific alternative the viewer is currently using.
The symptom
"Hooks aren't stopping the scroll"
Say this
"These hooks are stale" or "push harder" — Claude runs a hook diagnostic: self-resolving? stale language? third-person observation? hedging? Then rewrites against the specific problem.
The symptom
"No reason to believe this"
Say this
"Weak conviction" or "the viewer has no reason to believe" — Claude adds persona-specific barrier-busting: tenure details, social proof moments, sensory claims with evidence.
The symptom
"Nothing's improving across rounds"
Say this
"We're iterating without improving — what's the actual bottleneck?" — Claude runs the full rubric to identify which dimension is the real weakness instead of wordsmithing the wrong thing.
If output quality degrades over a long session (20+ exchanges), start a new conversation. Claude's attention to library data and system rules fades in very long threads. A fresh session with the same brief resets quality. This isn't a workaround — it's the correct workflow.

Breaking down video ads

Claude can't watch video. Use this Gemini prompt to turn any video ad into structured data Claude can analyze, scale, or adapt.

Setup (once per Gemini session)

1
Turn on thinking mode
In Gemini, enable "Think" or "Deep Think" mode. This improves transcription accuracy and scene detection — especially for fast-cut ads.
2
Paste the prompt below
Copy it into your Gemini chat as the first message. This sets the output format.
3
Upload videos one at a time
After the prompt is set, upload each video as a follow-up message. One video per message for best accuracy.

The Gemini prompt

Role: You are an expert direct-response Creative Strategist analyzing video ads for a performance creative agency.

Task: Analyze each provided video ad and produce a structured scene-by-scene breakdown. If an asset is a duplicate of one already processed, reply with "Duplicate asset skipped."

## Output Format

Header Data:
* Estimated Duration: [X] seconds
* Hook Archetype: [e.g., Skeptic/Honesty, Specific Claim, Identity Hook, etc.]
* Primary Structure: [A (Narrative), B (Listicle), C (Rapid Proof), or D (Testimonial)]
* Primary Motivator: [Problem, Outcome, Deal, or Anti-Alternative]

Scene Table:
| Timestamp | Visual Description (No Commas) | On-Screen Text (OCR) | VO Script |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Talent enters frame; holds product | 50% OFF TODAY | "You won't believe this deal" |

## Rules
1. Scene Transitions: Use actual visual cuts for timestamps, not fixed intervals.
2. Visual Descriptions: Provide for ALL scenes (first 40s max). CRITICAL: Use semicolons instead of commas to prevent CSV errors.
3. VO/OCR: Capture exactly. If VO matches OCR, transcribe both and add "(Matches OCR)".
4. Limit: Stop at 40 seconds or the final scene, whichever comes first.

Goal: Create a high-fidelity data library that allows a team to understand the ad's "DNA" without needing to rewatch the video. Provide the table in a markdown code block.

After you have the breakdown

Paste it into the Factor Claude project. Use it to:

Scale"ADAPT this ad into the Mediterranean lane" + paste the breakdown
Analyze"Decompose this — what's the hook mechanism, structural engine, and copy engine?"
Compare"How does this compare to Asset #20?"
Check the hook archetype tag. Gemini's classification is a starting point — verify it matches your read before using it in Claude. If Gemini misses VO text, replay the video at 0.5x and correct the transcript manually. Accurate VO is the most important field.
